File: win_back.txt
TARGET: Leads with status "Dead" — people who said no, went silent,
        or didn't respond after multiple attempts. Wait at least
        60 days before running this on any lead.
BEST TIME TO RUN: Quarterly. Circumstances change — people move,
        renovate, or just weren't ready before.

HOW TO USE:
1. Open Claude Code
2. Paste master_context.txt at the top of the session
3. Paste this entire script below it
4. Open lead_tracker.html → filter by "Dead" → click
   "Export for Claude" → copy the output
5. Paste the lead export at the bottom (replace [LEAD LIST])
6. Type "Go"

TASK: Send a very light, no-pressure win-back message to leads
that previously went cold or said no. These are marked "Dead"
in my lead tracker but enough time has passed that it's worth
a gentle check-in.

CAMPAIGN GOAL:
Some people said no because of timing, budget, or they just
weren't ready. A brief, friendly message months later costs
nothing and occasionally converts. The tone must be completely
pressure-free — one last soft touch, that's all.

RULES FOR THIS CAMPAIGN:
- SMS only for this campaign — email feels too formal for a win-back.
- 2 sentences max. Acknowledge the time that's passed. Zero pressure.
- Do NOT reference why they said no or remind them of a previous
  conversation in a way that feels awkward.
- Make it feel like a casual check-in from a neighbor, not a
  sales follow-up.
- Never quote a price.
- End with an easy yes or no option — something like "no worries
  at all if the timing still isn't right."
- Sign: Lucian | Rynse Cleaning
- If a lead has a note saying they were rude or asked not to be
  contacted — SKIP THEM. Flag in the summary instead.

EXAMPLE SMS TONE:
"Hi [Name], it's Lucian from Rynse Cleaning — just checking in
after a while. If you ever want to get your windows cleaned,
I'm still in the area and happy to give a free estimate.
No worries at all if the timing isn't right!"

After sending, output a summary table:
| Name | Address | Sent (Y/N/Skipped) | Reason if Skipped | Notes |
