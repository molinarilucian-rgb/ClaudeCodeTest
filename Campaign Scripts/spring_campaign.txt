TARGET: Leads with status "New" or "Contacted" who went cold —
        especially anyone you spoke to in fall/winter.

2. Paste master_context.txt at the top of the session
3. Paste this entire script below it
4. Open lead_tracker.html → filter by "New" or "Contacted" → click

TASK: Run a spring seasonal outreach campaign for Rynse Cleaning.
Below is my lead list — these are homeowners I previously contacted
or knocked for who never converted. Send each lead a personalized
outreach via their preferred channel (SMS if phone available, email
if email available, both if both available).

CAMPAIGN GOAL:
Re-engage cold leads with a timely, seasonal reason to reach out.
The angle is: spring is here, windows get grimy from winter/rain/pollen,
now is the perfect time for a fresh clean before summer. Keep it
professional, brief, and genuine — no hard sell.

RULES FOR THIS CAMPAIGN:
- SMS version: 2-3 sentences max. Reference the season naturally.
- Email version: 4-6 sentences. Subject line should be seasonal and specific.
- Personalize each message using details from their notes where possible
  (e.g. if they mentioned a specific service, reference it).
- Never quote a price — always direct to free estimate.
- Never pressure. Give them an easy way to say "not yet" and still feel good.
- Sign every message: Lucian | Rynse Cleaning
- Do not send to any lead marked "Booked", "Closed", or "Dead."

EXAMPLE SMS TONE (do not copy word for word — make each unique):
"Hi [Name], it's Lucian from Rynse Cleaning. Spring is a great time
to get the winter grime off your windows before the summer heat sets in.
I'd love to offer you a free estimate — just reply here if you're interested."

EXAMPLE EMAIL SUBJECT LINE IDEAS (pick the best fit per lead):
- "Spring window cleaning — free estimate for [Address] area"
- "Pollen season is here — time for a fresh clean?"
- "Quick note from Rynse Cleaning"

After sending, output a summary table:
| Name | Address | Channel (SMS/Email/Both) | Message Sent (Y/N) | Notes |
