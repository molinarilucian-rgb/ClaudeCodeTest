TARGET: Leads with status "Quote Sent" who haven't responded
        in 3+ days.
BEST TIME TO RUN: Every few days as needed. Check your tracker
        for anyone sitting in "Quote Sent" for 3+ days.

HOW TO USE:
1. Open Claude Code
2. Paste master_context.txt at the top of the session
3. Paste this entire script below it
4. Open lead_tracker.html → filter by "Quote Sent" → look at
   "Last Updated" column — flag anyone 3+ days old
5. Export for Claude → paste at the bottom (replace [LEAD LIST])
6. Tell Claude which leads are at Day 3 and which are at Day 7
7. Type "Go"

NOTE ON THE TWO-STEP SEQUENCE:
- Day 3 (first nudge): SMS only — brief, warm, "just checking in"
- Day 7 (second nudge): Email — slightly more urgency with a
  real reason ("I have an opening this week")
- After Day 7 with no response: Move to "Dead" in the tracker

TASK: Send quote follow-up messages to leads who received an
estimate but haven't responded.

For each lead, I will tell you if they are at Day 3 or Day 7
after receiving their quote. Use the correct message for each.

CAMPAIGN GOAL:
People get busy. A quote sitting in their inbox isn't a "no" —
it's just unfinished. These gentle nudges remind them without
making them feel pressured. The Day 7 message adds a real reason
to act (scheduling window) without being pushy or fake.

RULES — DAY 3 (SMS only):
- 2 sentences max. Warm check-in, zero pressure.
- Reference that you sent them a quote recently.
- Leave the door open without pushing.
- Sign: Lucian | Rynse Cleaning

RULES — DAY 7 (Email):
- 4-5 sentences. Slightly more concrete.
- Mention that you have a scheduling opening this week or coming week.
- This creates gentle urgency without being salesy.
- Offer to adjust the quote if anything has changed.
- Subject line should reference the estimate or their address.
- Sign: Lucian | Rynse Cleaning
- After this email: if still no response, move them to "Dead."

EXAMPLE DAY 3 SMS TONE:
"Hi [Name], it's Lucian from Rynse Cleaning — just following up
on the estimate I sent over. Happy to answer any questions,
no pressure at all!"

EXAMPLE DAY 7 EMAIL SUBJECT LINE IDEAS:
- "Scheduling opening this week — following up on your estimate"
- "Still available for your window cleaning — [Address]"
- "Quick follow-up on your Rynse Cleaning quote"

EXAMPLE DAY 7 EMAIL TONE:
"Hi [Name], I wanted to follow up one more time on the estimate
I sent for your home on [Address]. I have a scheduling opening
this week and wanted to check in before it fills up. If anything
has changed or you have questions about the quote, I'm happy to
talk it through. No pressure either way — just wanted to make
sure you had everything you need. Thanks for considering Rynse
Cleaning! — Lucian"

After sending, output a summary table:
| Name | Address | Day (3 or 7) | Channel | Sent (Y/N) | Next Action |
